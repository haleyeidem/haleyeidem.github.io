01Google Cloud2019 – 2023

Odonata

An internal data visualization tool that summarized user behavior at scale across the entire Google Cloud Platform, using machine learning to turn hundreds of millions of logged sessions into legible patterns, segments, and journeys.

I designed and built it as a researcher who believes an insight only counts if people can see it. Odonata was adopted by 300+ stakeholders for roadmap planning and became a shared visual language for how GCP understood its users.

03Personal · Minnesota Timberwolves2025 – 26 season

Shot Forest

An interactive shot chart for the Minnesota Timberwolves — planted as a forest. Each tree grows where a player takes a shot: size encodes volume, and a five-step green ramp encodes the player's FG% against the league's FG% from that exact spot on the floor.

NBA Stats data via nba_api, hexbinned in Python, rendered as hand-rolled SVG in a single static page — no backend, no D3, hosted on GitHub Pages.

04Personal · built for one very good catongoing

Dill's Litter Log

My cat Dill has IBD and FIC, which makes his litter box habits medically important data. So I built him a mobile app: one-tap logging into Firestore, with a year of history behind it.

It's a full analytics suite for an audience of one — scatter plots of events by date and time of day, a 24h × 7-day time-of-day heatmap, rolling trend lines, gap analysis with warning thresholds, and anomaly detection against Dill's own behavioral baseline. The same rigor I'd bring to product telemetry, pointed at a litter box.

fig. 05 · about

A researcher by training, a designer by instinct.

I'm a data storyteller with a combination of expertise in quantitative research, data visualization, and design. My favorite work sits exactly at the seam: statistically grounded reasoning with a researcher's attention to detail and a designer's attention to aesthetics.

I earned a PhD in computational genomics at Vanderbilt, after a decade across molecular biology, clinical genetics, genetic counseling, and bioinformatics — including research fellowships at the NIH and training at Johns Hopkins. Genetics taught me to find signal in some of the messiest data there is.

At Google Cloud, I spent seven years as a Quantitative UX Researcher — building GCP's first cross-product customer-journey measurement across hundreds of millions of sessions, ML-driven behavioral segmentation, and the executive reporting leaders used to set direction for products serving 13M+ users. Now at DigitalOcean, I lead analytics and strategy for new accounts as a multi-disciplinary product manager.

Alongside all of it: years of practice in art, color theory, graphic design, and digital illustration. The charts are better because of the paintings.
